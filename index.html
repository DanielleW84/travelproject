WHY LONDON?

London, the City of Seven Hills

Though not as famous as Rome's seven hills, London is often referred to as the "City of Seven Hills," a title that evokes the city's rich history and varied topography. Like Rome, London is built upon several elevated areas that have shaped the city's development over time. While the exact number of hills is debated, there are many notable rises in the landscape that have been part of London's story since its Roman days.

Some of the hills traditionally linked to London's iconic landscape include Parliament Hill in Hampstead Heath, Primrose Hill near Regent's Park, and Shooter's Hill in Southeast London. These hills, along with others like Highgate Hill and Sydenham Hill, not only offer breathtaking views of the sprawling city but also provide insight into London's ancient past, when the Romans chose elevated ground to build their settlement, Londinium.

Today, while the hills themselves are not as prominent as they once were, they still offer a glimpse of the city's ancient geography, where the blend of urban development and nature creates a unique sense of place. Whether you're standing on one of these hills or strolling through the vibrant streets below, London's hills remind us of the deep historical layers that define this magnificent city.
